Rover With Arm

Version 1 — Wi-Fi controlled rover with a small robotic arm

← Back to projects

Project Overview

Rover With Arm is a wheeled rover designed to move in multiple directions while carrying a small mechanical arm on top.

The purpose of this robot was to hand me objects during a video interview for the InCube Challenge organized by the ETH Zurich Entrepreneurship Club. The interview was successful and allowed me to move on to the next stage of the selection process, which consisted of three rounds.

Prototype Gallery

Mechanical Design

The base of the rover was recovered from a toy I had as a child. It uses two RS380 DC motors for movement.

The robotic arm is 3D-printed and uses three micro servos, allowing it to move in different directions and perform simple predefined gestures.

Control System

This version is remotely controlled from a smartphone using the Wi-Fi capabilities of the Arduino UNO WiFi. By connecting the phone to the Arduino's Wi-Fi network, the user can control both the movement of the rover and the actions of the robotic arm.

In addition to manual movement control, the arm can execute predefined actions, such as “hand over the object” or “wave”.

Main Components

  • Recovered toy rover base
  • Two RS380 DC motors
  • 3D-printed robotic arm
  • Three micro servos for arm movement
  • Arduino UNO WiFi
  • Wi-Fi control from smartphone
  • Predefined robotic arm actions

Purpose of the Prototype

The goal of this project was to create a simple but functional robot capable of interacting physically with its environment during a real scenario. In this case, the rover was used to deliver objects during an interview, combining mobility, remote control, and basic robotic manipulation.

Even though this was an early version, it represented an important step in experimenting with mobile robotics, 3D-printed mechanisms, and human-robot interaction.
